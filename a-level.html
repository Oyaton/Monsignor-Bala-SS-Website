A-Level Subjects

Advanced Level Curriculum

The A-Level program at Monsignor Bala Secondary School is designed to prepare students for university education and future careers. Students specialize in their chosen subject combinations while developing advanced knowledge and skills.

Our A-Level curriculum follows the Uganda Advanced Certificate of Education (UACE) syllabus, with additional enrichment activities to enhance learning. Students typically study three principal subjects and two subsidiary subjects.

We offer guidance to help students select subject combinations that align with their interests, strengths, and future career aspirations.

Subject Combinations

Science Combinations

PCM: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics

This combination is ideal for students interested in engineering, computer science, architecture, and other technical fields.

Physics (PHY): Advanced mechanics, electricity and magnetism, thermodynamics, waves, and modern physics
Chemistry (CHEM): Physical chemistry, organic chemistry, inorganic chemistry, and analytical methods
Mathematics (MTC): Pure mathematics, mechanics, statistics, and calculus
PCB: Physics, Chemistry, Biology

This combination is suitable for students pursuing careers in medicine, pharmacy, veterinary science, and other health-related fields.

Physics (PHY): Focus on mechanics, electricity, and radiation physics
Chemistry (CHEM): Emphasis on biochemistry and organic chemistry
Biology (BIO): Advanced cell biology, genetics, human physiology, and ecology
BCM: Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics

This combination prepares students for careers in biotechnology, environmental science, biostatistics, and related fields.

Biology (BIO): Focus on molecular biology, genetics, and ecology
Chemistry (CHEM): Emphasis on biochemistry and analytical chemistry
Mathematics (MTC): Focus on statistics, calculus, and mathematical modeling

Arts Combinations

HEG: History, Economics, Geography

This combination is ideal for students interested in social sciences, international relations, law, and public administration.

History (HIST): Advanced African history, European history, and historical research methods
Economics: Microeconomics, macroeconomics, development economics, and international trade
Geography (GEO): Advanced physical geography, human geography, and geographical techniques
HEL: History, Economics, Literature

This combination prepares students for careers in journalism, publishing, education, and cultural studies.

History (HIST): Focus on political and cultural history
Economics: Emphasis on economic theory and policy
Literature: Advanced literary analysis, African literature, and world literature
GEM: Geography, Economics, Mathematics

This combination is suitable for students interested in urban planning, environmental management, statistics, and economic analysis.

Geography (GEO): Focus on economic geography and environmental management
Economics: Emphasis on quantitative economics and econometrics
Mathematics (MTC): Focus on statistics, mathematical modeling, and applied mathematics

Subsidiary Subjects

General Paper

General Paper is a compulsory subsidiary subject for all A-Level students. It develops critical thinking, analytical skills, and effective communication through essay writing and comprehension exercises.

Key Topics:
  • Current affairs and contemporary issues
  • Science and technology
  • Environment and sustainable development
  • Ethics and social responsibility
  • Culture and society

The General Paper assessment includes essay writing, comprehension exercises, and data response questions.

Information & Communication Technology (ICT)

ICT is offered as a subsidiary subject to develop digital literacy and technical skills that are essential in today's technology-driven world.

Key Topics:
  • Advanced computer applications
  • Database management
  • Web design and development
  • Information systems
  • Computer programming fundamentals

Students have access to our modern computer laboratory for practical sessions, and assessments include both theoretical and practical components.

University Pathways

Our A-Level program is designed to prepare students for university education and future careers. The subject combinations we offer align with various university degree programs and career paths.

Science Combinations Lead to:
  • Medicine and health sciences
  • Engineering and technology
  • Computer science and information technology
  • Architecture and built environment
  • Environmental science and natural resources
Arts Combinations Lead to:
  • Law and legal studies
  • Business and economics
  • Social sciences and humanities
  • Education and teaching
  • Journalism and communication

Our career guidance team provides support to help students make informed decisions about university applications and career choices.

Assessment and Examinations

Continuous Assessment

Throughout the A-Level program, students' progress is monitored through various forms of continuous assessment.

  • Regular classwork and homework assignments
  • Class presentations and group projects
  • Laboratory practical work and field studies
  • Quizzes and topic tests
  • Research papers and essays

Formal Examinations

Formal examinations are conducted at regular intervals to assess students' understanding and application of knowledge.

  • Mid-term examinations in each term
  • End-of-term examinations
  • Mock examinations in the final year (S6)
  • Uganda Advanced Certificate of Education (UACE) examinations
  • Practical examinations for science subjects
Academic Support

We provide comprehensive academic support to help students excel in their A-Level studies:

  • Extra tutoring sessions for challenging subjects
  • Study groups facilitated by teachers
  • Access to library and online resources
  • Regular feedback on academic progress
  • Examination preparation workshops
  • University application guidance
